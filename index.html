      PaleoView

      is a proposed alternate front-end to the Neotoma Paleoecological Database. It provides a cohesive visual interface for exploring trends in space, time, and abundance for a variety of taxa included in Neotoma. Using the temporal navigator in the righthand panel, users can gain an understanding of how the abundance of a particular taxonomic group has changed through time since the Last Glacial Maximum (~21,000 years before the present). The map interface shows a proportional symbol representation of individually dated sites with symbol radii scaled to the relative abundance of the chosen taxon at that point in time. PaleoView represents a significant improvement over the existing Neotoma solution, allowing to explore in greater detail -- yielding improved identification of patterns trends in both space and time. To this end, PaleoView supports a greater range of interaction, including contextual overlays and resymbolization to improve data exploration.
